Social Hotspots

Not every Vegas trip is about rest — some are about going full throttle. These Social Hotspots are built for groups, nightlife, dayclubs, DJs, and everything in between. If your trip is about energy, late nights, and high-volume fun, these are your go-to hotels.

Best Party Hotels in Las Vegas

The Cosmopolitan of Las Vegas ★★★★★

Price per night: $269

  • Rooftop pool parties and lounges
  • Walkable location on the Strip
  • Stylish rooms for groups

The Cosmopolitan is the social hotel. From daybeds at the pool to DJs at Marquee, it’s built for movement. You’ll find curated cocktails, skyline views, and an energetic crowd almost anytime of day. It’s perfect for weekenders and celebration trips.

Virgin Hotels Las Vegas ★★★★☆

Price per night: $179

  • Outdoor pool events and daylife scene
  • Live music, lounges, and rooftop spaces
  • No resort fees

Virgin Hotels feels like it was made for friends traveling together. The layout is open and social, the entertainment spaces host frequent events, and the energy never drops. It’s easy to see why it’s a regular choice for bachelor and bachelorette groups.

View Hotel

Resorts World Las Vegas ★★★★☆

Price per night: $245

  • Three hotels under one roof
  • Zouk Nightclub and Ayu Dayclub
  • Cutting-edge design and tech

Resorts World gives you everything in one place: parties, pools, food, and fashion. Zouk is one of the most high-profile clubs in Vegas right now, and the Ayu Dayclub brings tropical vibes to the desert. The rooms are sleek, and everything is built for a full-scale social trip.

The LINQ Hotel + Experience ★★★★☆

Price per night: $149

  • Great for younger crowds and groups
  • Pool with games, DJs, and cabanas
  • Next to LINQ Promenade nightlife

The LINQ is approachable, affordable, and never boring. With beer pong tables by the pool, easy access to bars, and a crowd that’s there for the fun, it’s ideal for group travel. Plus, you're right next to the High Roller and late-night taco spots.

SAHARA Las Vegas ★★★★☆

Price per night: $159

  • Stylish rooms with a modern twist
  • On-site lounges and music-driven spaces
  • Close to downtown and the Strip

SAHARA offers an upscale vibe without the price tag. It’s home to Azilo Ultra Pool and lounges that keep things going into the night. The property blends a relaxed energy with easy access to both Fremont and the main Strip.

MGM Grand Hotel & Casino ★★★★☆

Price per night: $179

  • Hakkasan nightclub on-site
  • Huge resort with bars and lounges
  • Concerts and live events regularly

MGM Grand has been a staple for party weekends for years — and with good reason. Hakkasan remains a heavyweight in the Vegas club scene, and the property often hosts major music events and festivals. There’s always something happening.

Planet Hollywood Resort & Casino ★★★★☆

Price per night: $199

  • Rooftop pool deck with Strip views
  • Miracle Mile shopping and nightlife access
  • Energetic casino atmosphere

Planet Hollywood sits in the middle of the action and brings a younger, more casual vibe. It's an easy pick for birthdays, reunions, or impulsive getaways. Rooms are large, and everything from bars to shows is within reach.

Tropicana Las Vegas ★★★★☆

Price per night: $139

  • Laid-back pool with party potential
  • Short walk to MGM and Excalibur nightlife
  • Affordable option with open layout

Tropicana offers a more relaxed party vibe — think casual cocktails and open outdoor space. It’s an affordable base with room to spread out, perfect for groups that want flexibility without losing the Vegas feel.
